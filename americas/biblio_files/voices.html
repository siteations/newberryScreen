    The Newberry holds a unique collection of printed and manuscript materials related to indigenous languages. Much of it is included in the collection of Edward E. Ayer, a 19th-century Midwestern businessman whose interest in contact moments between indigenous people and Europeans inspired him to collect many handwritten manuscripts and early printed works produced by the church in New Spain. Indigenous-language materials are also prevalent in the William B. Greenlee Collection, focused on Portuguese and Brazilian history, and in the Prince Louis Lucien Bonaparte Collection, focused on historical linguistics. For further information on these collections and how to use them, follow the catalog links in the resource or contact a Newberry Reference Librarian.
