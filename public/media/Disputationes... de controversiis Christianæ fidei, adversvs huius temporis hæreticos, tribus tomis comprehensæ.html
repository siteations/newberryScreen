    1. 1 2017-06-15T21:27:48+00:00 Controversial Divines 8 plain 2017-07-18T15:19:43+00:00
      Theological disagreements between divines being common in the Middle Ages, there was nothing terribly new at first about Luther challenging the Church—namely, its indulgences. Once whole cities and territories began converting to the evangelical faith, however, it became clear that this was no ordinary dispute between divines. Already in the 1520s the difference between the survival or collapse of a church (or in some cases a state) depended in large part on how well its divines defended their own understanding of God and refuted that of others.
      Constantly on the defensive against a seemingly endless wave of criticism in these years, Catholic divines struggled to formulate a coherent strategy for fighting back against and halting the advance of the Protestant churches. To make things more difficult, Protestant theology was still taking form at this time, which left Catholics without a single firm target.
      The tide turned by the end of the century, when Protestant doctrines had solidified to the point where they could be more effectively refuted. Now it was possible for Catholic divines to produce studies like Roberto Bellarmino’s Disputations About the Controversies of the Christian Faith. In this massive, multi-volume work, Bellarmino systematically presented and offered refutations of the major theological writers from the Protestant side. His principal target was Calvin, the main subject of the chapter shown here, in which Bellarmino promises to “break down” all of his opponent’s major arguments.
      Bellarmino’s work brought great comfort to the Catholic Church, but it came so late that it had no chance of eradicating Protestantism, no matter how well Catholic divines argued. This reality did not stop Catholic and Protestant divines from engaging in controversy throughout our period and beyond.
